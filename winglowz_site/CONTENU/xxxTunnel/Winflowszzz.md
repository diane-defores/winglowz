## Timeboxing

Assigning fixed time frames to tasks can significantly boost productivity and focus.

Hello,

Today, I've got something special for you: a time management trick used by the likes of Elon Musk and Bill Gates. 

But let’s pause for a moment. Think back to a day when you had a crazy tight deadline. 

Remember the adrenaline? How you magically finished it? 

Now contrast that with a lazy Saturday where your only plan was to vacuum. 
It took all day, didn’t it? Funny how that works.

The time management trick leverages exactly that phenomenon.

It's called timeboxing.

Timeboxing is simple. You limit a task to a fixed, realistic timeline. 

Why does this work? Temporal motivation theory tells us our focus sharpens when we value our time in relation to a deadline. 

In essence, deadlines are powerful motivators.

Elon Musk, for instance, takes timeboxing to the extreme. 

He divides his day into 5-minute slots, each for a specific task. 

This level of granularity helps him juggle his roles at SpaceX and Tesla. 

He starts with technical discussions, shifts to managerial tasks, and fits in unplanned issues. 

He even combines meals with meetings to maximize his time. 

It’s intense, and while it works for him, remember: balance is key to avoid burnout.

You don’t have to go to Elon's lengths to see benefits. Here’s how to start timeboxing effectively:

1. Set Your Timebox for Each Task

The first step is about being intentional with your time. 

Estimate how long you think each task will take. It's crucial to be realistic to avoid setting yourself up for failure. 

1. Use the Time Flow System

It's not just about blocking time; it's about aligning your tasks with your goals and priorities. 

To make the most of it, create Focus Blocks. These are the core of the [Time Flow System](https://sy.lifehack.org/l/uNrwrCdqN892nH22lGQ763uZpQ/PPXZIRyZjjIGW892iY0Y18pg/gH8925ZBA73eaoMxqMiOwyxg). 

![](https://ci3.googleusercontent.com/meips/ADKq_NZZYzfY8AgBoFIMtrVUhsfBAJ--awC_PbsRcY22udSWU-PheoLMuafN5MiYRCE-IVhZTnYmF_mbPmH_ktJyCVxOlbqJ=s0-d-e1-ft#https://sy.lifehack.org/uploads/1702449220.png)

Each Focus Block is dedicated to a specific task or group of tasks. 

You define two things for each block: the action(s) you'll perform and the time you'll spend on them. 

This isn't just random to-do list items; these actions are chosen because they align with your bigger picture goals.

Ask yourself, "Does this task move me closer to my goals?" If yes, it earns a place in your Focus Block.

1. Match Tasks with Your Energy Level

Your energy levels fluctuate throughout the day. 

Some tasks, like creative work, might require your peak energy times, while others, like administrative tasks, can be done when your energy dips. 

Plan your day accordingly, and don't forget to include time for rest and rejuvenation.

1. Review and Adjust

After completing your tasks, reflect on the process. 

Did you allocate enough time? Were there unexpected interruptions? 

Use these insights to refine your timeboxing technique for future tasks.

Timeboxing isn't just about doing more; it's about doing what matters. It’s a simple, yet incredibly effective way to manage your time and energy. 

Start mastering your schedule like the pros.

Cheers,

Leon

## Distractions = #1 Killer of Productivity

Whether external interruptions, unconscious thoughts, or unclear priorities, can significantly hinder our focus

Hello,

Did you know that on average, a person gets distracted every 11 minutes? 

That's startling, right? And regaining focus? It takes about 25 minutes. 

This is not just a minor hiccup - it means roughly 6 hours of your 8-hour workday might be slipping away due to distractions.

You've probably heard it before: 

"Distractions are productivity's biggest enemy." 

But are you fully aware of just how deep this impact goes? 

Consider these figures….

On a personal level:

54% underperform due to distractions.

50% experience a significant drop in productivity.

20% find their career growth stunted.

At an organizational level, distractions can lead to:

45% reduced quality of work.

30% lower team morale.

25% strain in boss-employee relationships.

24% missed deadlines.

21% direct loss in revenue.

Distractions, as much as we dislike them, are a part of life. 

It's not about creating an impossible, distraction-free world. Our world is dynamic, after all. 

The real goal is to manage these distractions effectively. 

By doing so, you can boost your productivity, meet your goals, and maybe even find some peace amid the noise.

#### Macros Can Automate Any Task

Macros are recordings of any mouse movements, keyboard taps, and mouse clicks under one command. Any time you want to repeat all of the actions performed the first time, you can click once to repeat the command. The software repeats whatever you have programmed it to do.

#### Qu'est-ce Qu'un Enregistreur de Macros ?

Un **enregistreur de macros**, comme son titre l'indique, est un outil qui enregistre les macros. Une macro est une séquence d'actions de l'utilisateur (mouvements de souris, frappes, clics, etc.) qui peut être rejouée plus tard comme un "script". Le but premier de tout enregistreur de macros est de faire le travail à la place d'un humain. C'est un robot qui exécute toutes les actions répétitives que tu lui as demandées.

Voici une liste des fonctions "indispensables" que tout bon programme de macro doit posséder :

* Enregistrement de la souris et du clavier
* Vitesse de lecture variable
* Éditeur de macros qui permet d'ajuster un script enregistré.
* "Yeux" - il doit être capable de trouver des images sur un écran, de détecter le déplacement d'une fenêtre, etc.
* Boucle - lecture en plusieurs fois

2. With the **Alt key** held down, type the four-digit code on the numeric keypad for the character you want. (Include the leading 0 if that’s required)**Note:** This only works on the numeric keypad. This won’t work on the row of numbers at the top of the keyboard
