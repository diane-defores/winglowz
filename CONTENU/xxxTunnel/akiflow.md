The Command Bar is the **fastest way to create and edit tasks/events** in Akiflow. You can open it from anywhere on your laptop.

## ⌨️ How to open it?

The default shortcut to open it is `ALT/OPT` `SPACE`, but it can be changed in **Settings → Shortcuts**:

*_Ctrl for Windows users_

## 🆕 Create tasks and events

### **Create a task ☑️**

With the command bar opened, you can type the task's name and just hit `ENTER` to add the task straight to the Inbox.

### **Create an event 🗓️**

With the command bar opened you can type the event's name and use `ctrl/cmd + 2` to select the "New Event" option.

### **Create a Zoom meeting 🔵**

Simply type “Zoom”, and the command bar will prompt you to create a new meeting

## ✨ Special Characters

🦄

Use the **Special Characters** to add information to tasks and events quickly

You can find the special characters at the **bottom** of the **Command Bar**:

## 🔒 Lock your tasks

Locking your tasks, Akiflow **will schedule events on your Google Calendar**, so your colleagues will know you're on focus time and won't disturb the flow.

To do that, click on your tasks from the calendar, **hit the lock icon**, and select the calendar in which you want to lock your task.

![image](https://images.spr.so/cdn-cgi/imagedelivery/j42No7y-dcokJuNgXeA0ig/cc04bb7e-4706-4eac-9ddd-461109bc44ad/Screenshot_2023-12-08_at_3.15.04_PM/w=1080,quality=90,fit=scale-down)

You can then set up your preferences for **event visibility:**

- 📢 **Public:** Anyone can see your task details, including the description.
- 🥸 **Private:** Your task details can't be seen by others unless they have at least the 'Make changes to events' permission for this calendar.
- 🚫 **Busy:** Your task title in Google Calendar will be “Busy” so not even the workspace admin can see your task title.

📣

Recurrent tasks will be locked in the calendar **15 instances in the future at a time**. You won't need to take further action.

## **🔥 Leverage** [![Time Slots](https://notion.so/icons/layers_purple.svg)Time Slots](https://how-to-use-guide.akiflow.com/time-slots) **for Effective Time Blocking**

Time Slots are not just a feature; they're a revolution in approaching time blocking. Why? Because they act as dedicated containers within your calendar, ensuring that every task has its rightful place.

**Why Use Time Slots?**

Time blocking is about allocating specific chunks of time to tasks, ensuring you stay on track. Time Slots take this a step further. They allow you to:

- Organize multiple tasks within specific periods, ensuring clarity.
- Set aside dedicated times for varied activities, be it admin tasks, marketing efforts, or personal needs.
- Visualize your day or week, making adjustments as needed.

### **⚡️ Use Time Slots Effectively**

1. **Design Your Week:** Open your calendar and start by creating Time Slots for all the activities you foresee. This could be work-related tasks, personal chores, or even leisure time.
2. **Assign Project:** Enhance your visual feedback by assigning projects to your Time Slots. Akiflow will automatically change the slot color into the project one, so you can quickly understand what you have to work on at a glance, making your calendar more intuitive and organized.
3. **Task Allocation:** Once your Time Slots are in place, begin allocating tasks to them. Remember, the appearance of a Slot isn't affected by the time, duration, or project of its tasks. It's all about organization.
4. **Recurring Needs:** Have a regular activity every Monday? Set the Time Slot to recur, ensuring you don't have to recreate it every time.
5. **Stay Flexible:** The beauty of Time Slots is the flexibility they offer. Need to adjust a Time Slot? Simply drag and drop. Want to edit tasks within a Slot? Use the Command Bar or right-click options.
6. ******************Pin Slots:****************** Want to alleviate distractions and increase focus? Simply Pin the slots using `F` to open a separate column and work on tasks only in that slot.

# Statistics Overview

**Key Features:**

- Shows statistics for your schedule, tasks, and events within aif flow
- Schedule section displays:
  - Done tasks and time spent
  - Trend line for task load, events, and meetings
- Task section categorizes data by:
  - Projects
  - Tags
  - Sources (integrations with other tools)
- Events section provides:
  - Total events and time spent
  - Timeline view with internal, external, and solo events
  - Internal events are for teams set up within aif flow
  - External events are calendar meetings with others
  - Solo events are calendar events with yourself

**Observations and Suggestions:**

- Ability to download statistics as a report or PDF would be useful
- Option to toggle event timeline to show days, hours, or minutes
- Clarification needed on how time tracking works across different sources/tools
- Accuracy of metrics may be impacted when using aif flow and other tools simultaneously

**Additional Details:**

- Statistics build up over time as you use aif flow and connected tools
- Customizable date range selection available
- Hover over chart elements to view specific data points
- Provides visibility into time spent across projects, tags, and integrated sources

Overall, the aif flow statistics feature offers insights into your productivity, task management, and time allocation within the tool and connected apps. Enhancements like download options, time tracking clarification, and timeline customization could further improve the user experience.
