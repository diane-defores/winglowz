### plan for worse case scenario / prepare to fail to not fail because you failed to prepare!

This morning I was getting ready to go somewhere and noticed I only had about 90 miles left in my gas tank.

My destination was about 35 miles away. Gas light goes off around 60 miles. According to my calculations, I might be able to arrive to my destination without turning on my gas light.

I thought about it for a second and just planned for worse case scenario and filled up my tank before I left in the cold.

By doing this, it allowed me to focus on other important issues during that drive.

Had I done it later, I would have been focusing on my gas bar the entire drive WHILE looking for gas stations to stop at. So much energy wasted for a little task.

For the company I run, I do not do any of the work. I'm more of the thinker behind the strategy/creative.

In order for me to get amazing ideas on a daily basis, I have to try my best to live stress free and the best way to do that is to plan for worst case scenario.

Planning for worse case scenario gives you a birds eye view of any situation. And the best part is, doesn't get any worse. Either it works or you default to your worse case scenario.

Here go a few areas that I apply this to.

- Finances
- Meeting people
- Projects
- Clothing (rain or shine)
- Oversleeping

Planning worse case scenario will also decrease the chances of you having to execute that plan because you will beable to focus on your end goal without worrying wasting energy on the uncertainty of failure.

Flow is achieved best under the right amount of pressure and security. Liberty leads to creativity

### Microproductivité vs. Macroproductivité

 🌟 La macroproductivité se concentre sur le résultat global d'un système de productivité.
 💡 La microproductivité concerne l'efficacité lors de la réalisation de tâches individuelles.
 📱 Réduire les distractions, telles que les notifications de téléphone, est crucial pour améliorer la microproductivité.
 🤔 Faire des pauses intelligentes lors des tâches permet de maintenir la concentration et d'optimiser la réflexion (comme le sommeil)
 ⏳ Aller au bout d'un projet avant de commencer à l'exécuter aide le cerveau à anticiper les étapes suivantes, favorisant ainsi la productivité.
 💪 Combinaison de la macro et de la microproductivité pour obtenir des résultats efficaces dans la gestion du temps.
 🎯 Consacrer régulièrement du temps à des tâches spécifiques chaque jour améliore la productivité micro.
 📚 La répétition et la discipline quotidienne sont essentielles pour maximiser la productivité à long terme.
 Exercice Pratique:
   Recommandation de dédier au moins 45 minutes par jour à une tâche spécifique, en coupant les distractions, pour améliorer la microproductivité.
 Conclusion:
   La combinaison efficace de la micro et macroproductivité peut conduire à des accomplissements significatifs.

Productivity is a concept that has been studied and discussed by many authors and experts in the field of personal development and time management. Here is a list of some of the most well-known authors and pionners in the field of productivity:

**David Allen** - Author of the best-selling book "Getting Things Done," David Allen is considered one of the pioneers of modern productivity. His system, known as GTD, focuses on capturing all tasks and commitments in a trusted system, processing them into actionable items, and reviewing them regularly to ensure progress.

**Stephen Covey** - Author of the classic book "The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People," Stephen Covey emphasizes the importance of prioritizing tasks based on their importance and urgency. His time management matrix, also known as the Eisenhower Matrix, helps individuals identify and focus on tasks that are both important and urgent.

**Tim Ferriss** - Author of "The 4-Hour Workweek," Tim Ferriss challenges traditional notions of work and productivity. He advocates for eliminating non-essential tasks, automating processes, and outsourcing work to increase efficiency and create more time for personal pursuits.

**Cal Newport** - Author of "Deep Work," Cal Newport emphasizes the importance of focused, uninterrupted work for achieving high levels of productivity. He argues that distractions and multitasking can hinder productivity, and advocates for creating blocks of time for deep, focused work.

**Brian Tracy** - Author of "Eat That Frog!," Brian Tracy emphasizes the importance of tackling the most important and challenging tasks first, known as "eating the frog." This approach helps individuals overcome procrastination and make progress on their most important goals.

**Charles Duhigg** - Author of "The Power of Habit," Charles Duhigg explores the science behind habits and how they can be changed to increase productivity. He argues that understanding the habit loop (cue, routine, reward) can help individuals break bad habits and form new, productive ones.

**Angela Duckworth** - Author of "Grit," Angela Duckworth emphasizes the importance of perseverance and passion for achieving long-term goals. She argues that talent alone is not enough for success, and that individuals who are gritty and persistent are more likely to achieve their goals.

These authors and many others have contributed to our understanding of productivity and offered valuable insights and strategies for improving our time management and work habits. While their approaches may differ, they all emphasize the importance of focus, prioritization, and discipline for achieving high levels of productivity.

## The Pillars of Productivity
### Macro and micro productivity
Understanding the balance between macro and micro productivity is essential for achieving sustainable success. Macro productivity focuses on long-term goals and outcomes, while micro productivity emphasizes daily tasks and habits that contribute to overall progress. By harmonizing these two aspects, you can maximize your productivity potential and drive significant accomplishments.
### Tailor your agenda to your energy levels
Adapting your agenda to your energy levels is a strategic approach to optimizing your productivity. By identifying your peak performance times and aligning your tasks accordingly, you can enhance your focus, efficiency, and overall effectiveness. Tailoring your agenda to your energy levels allows you to work smarter, not harder, and achieve better results with less effort.
### Personalize your work to your objectives
Personalizing your workflows to align with your objectives is a key factor in achieving success. By customizing your processes, tools, and strategies to suit your specific goals and preferences, you can streamline your workflow, increase your motivation, and enhance your overall productivity.
