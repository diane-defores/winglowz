# Business Context

## Mission

WinGlows exists to help Windows users build practical, repeatable productivity systems, with `Windows Mastery` as the commercial anchor.

## Business Anchor

- `Windows Mastery` is the flagship offer and primary narrative center.
- Bilingual content (`en` and `fr`) supports discovery, qualification, and conversion.
- Gated documentation and learning surfaces extend the paid experience after account and purchase flow.
- Companion product pages extend the ecosystem without replacing the flagship positioning.

## Business Model

- Free educational surfaces drive top-of-funnel trust and SEO.
- Conversion points route qualified users toward the flagship paid offer.
- Gated learning content supports post-purchase activation.
- Adjacent products remain supporting surfaces around the core offer.

This document does not assert revenue amounts, conversion rates, or market-share claims.

## Target Audience

Primary audience:

- Windows-first professionals and independents
- operators with recurring workflow complexity
- learners who prefer structured systems over generic tool lists

Secondary audience:

- educators and advanced learners documenting Windows workflows
- bilingual audiences requiring consistent `en` and `fr` value messaging

## Positioning Guardrails

- Keep WinGlows anchored to Windows workflow mastery, not generic productivity claims.
- Keep the commercial story centered on `Windows Mastery`.
- Avoid traction, market-size, or quantified outcome claims unless they are backed by in-repo evidence.
- Keep sister products as ecosystem context, not as the replacement narrative.

## Operational KPIs

- qualified traffic to Windows-intent pages
- conversion to `Windows Mastery` offer pages
- account creation and gated-content activation
- repeat consumption of premium learning surfaces
