Subject: The big problem is rarely the big disaster
Preview: The highest cost usually comes from repeated small frictions.
CTA: https://winflowz.com/windows-mastery

Most people imagine they lose time because of a few big visible problems.

In reality, what drains a day usually looks more like this:

- searching for a tab that is already open
- reopening information you already saw yesterday
- hesitating between several tools
- switching context too often
- absorbing interface noise, messages, and notifications

Taken one by one, each friction seems tiny.

But once you stack them across a full day, then a full week, you get:

- more fatigue
- less continuity
- less focus
- less desire to tackle the work that actually matters

The worst part is that this kind of friction starts to feel normal.

You get used to working against your environment.

You stop noticing how expensive it really is.

instead of layering more motivation on top, reduce the hidden cost of computer work.
